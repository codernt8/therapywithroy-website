A therapist who understands the weight of professional life

Before becoming a full-time therapist, I spent over 30 years in senior Human Resources roles across banking, aviation, luxury retail, and hospitality. I saw every day how people were expected to keep performing — to stay composed, capable, and driven — even while carrying enormous personal burdens.

That experience taught me something important: without genuine attention to mental health, it is very difficult to truly thrive in life. It’s what led me to retrain as a therapist.

I graduated with a Master in Social Sciences (Counselling) from the University of Hong Kong in 2017, and went on to volunteer as a counsellor working with clients facing family difficulties, relationship challenges, LGBT-related concerns, depression, anxiety, and career development issues.

Originally from Hong Kong, I relocated to the UK in 2021 and now work with clients online across the UK and internationally. I have a particular understanding of the cultural pressures that many East Asian and immigrant communities face, particularly the tension between family expectations and personal identity. I offer sessions in both English and Cantonese.

My approach is integrative and tailored to each individual. I draw on Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT), Emotion-Focused Therapy, and Bowen’s Family Systems Theory, combining evidence-based techniques with a warm, genuinely non-judgemental presence.

Have a question?

Here are some of the things people ask before getting started.

How does online therapy work? +

Sessions take place via Google Meet, a free, easy-to-use video platform. You'll receive a link before each session. All you need is a private space, a stable internet connection, and a device with a camera and microphone. Many clients find online therapy just as effective as in-person, with the added benefit of attending from the comfort of their own home.

What happens in the free consultation? +

The free 30-minute consultation is an informal, no-obligation conversation. We'll talk about what's brought you here, what you're hoping to get from therapy, and I'll answer any questions you have about how I work. It's also a chance for you to get a feel for whether we're the right fit — there's no pressure to proceed.

How many sessions will I need? +

Every person is different, and there's no fixed number of sessions. Some people find that a focused course of 6–12 sessions is enough to address a specific issue; others prefer longer-term support. We'll review how things are going together and you're free to end or pause at any time. There's no minimum commitment.

Is everything I share confidential? +

Yes. Everything you share is treated with the strictest confidence. The only exceptions are rare situations where there is a serious risk of harm to yourself or others — in those cases, I have a professional duty to act. I'll explain the full boundaries of confidentiality before we begin working together.

What is your cancellation policy? +

Payment is taken at the time of booking. If you need to reschedule or cancel, please give at least 48 hours' notice and you'll receive a full refund. Cancellations made within 48 hours of your appointment are non-refundable. This policy reflects the value of the time we've set aside together.

Do you work with clients outside the UK? +

Yes, I work with clients across the UK and internationally. Sessions are conducted entirely online, so location is no barrier. All fees are charged in GBP. If you're in a different time zone, we'll find a session time that works for both of us within my available hours (Monday to Friday, 9am–7pm UK time).

Are you a registered therapist? +

Yes. I am a registered member of the British Association for Counselling and Psychotherapy (BACP), membership number 397064. I hold a Master of Social Sciences in Counselling from the University of Hong Kong (2017) and practise in accordance with the BACP Ethical Framework for the Counselling Professions.
