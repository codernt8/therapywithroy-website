Couples Therapy

What Actually Happens in Couples Therapy?
(And Is It Right for Us?)

Roy Lam
MBACP Registered Therapist
7 March 2026 🕒 4 min read

Many couples arrive at therapy feeling a mixture of hope and nervousness. They want things to be different, but they’re not quite sure what they’re walking into. If that sounds familiar, this post is for you.

It’s a space for both of you — equally

Couples therapy isn’t about taking sides or deciding who’s right. From the very first session, my role as your therapist is to create a space where both partners feel genuinely heard. That means listening to each of your perspectives with equal attention, without judgment, and without agenda.

Most people are surprised by how quickly that feels like a relief.

What the first session actually looks like

The first session is largely about getting to know you — as individuals, and as a couple. We’ll talk about what’s brought you here, what you each hope for, and a little about your history together. I’ll explain how the sessions work, what confidentiality means, and what we might focus on going forward.

You don’t need to have all the answers. You don’t even need to agree on what the problem is. Starting the conversation is enough.

What we work on together

Every couple is different, but some of the most common themes I see are:

  • Communication that keeps breaking down — the same arguments, going nowhere
  • Emotional distance — feeling more like housemates than partners
  • Broken trust — whether from infidelity, secrets, or years of small disappointments
  • Life transitions — new jobs, having children, bereavement, or identity shifts that put pressure on the relationship
  • One partner wanting to leave — sometimes therapy helps clarify the path forward, even if that’s a difficult one

It’s not just for couples in crisis

One of the most common misconceptions is that therapy is a last resort. In reality, many couples come to me not because things are falling apart, but because they want to invest in their relationship before small issues become bigger ones. Think of it less like emergency care, and more like a regular MOT for something that matters to you.

Does it actually work?

Research consistently shows that couples therapy improves communication, rebuilds emotional intimacy, and increases relationship satisfaction. But the honest answer is: it works when both partners are willing to engage, even imperfectly. You don’t need to be ready — you just need to be willing to try.
