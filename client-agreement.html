Client Agreement

Version 2  |  March 2026

This agreement sets out how we will work together and outlines my professional obligations, including around confidentiality. At our first session I will set aside time to discuss this with you and answer any questions. You will receive a copy to sign electronically via DocuSeal before we begin working together.

Qualifications and Professional Membership

I hold an MSocSc in Counselling from the University of Hong Kong (2017). I am a Registered Member of the British Association for Counselling and Psychotherapy (BACP), Membership No. 397064, and abide by their Ethical Framework.

Anti-Discriminatory Practice

I am committed to providing an anti-discriminatory service and strive to ensure this is present in all our work together. I welcome clients of all backgrounds, cultures, identities, and orientations.

The Way I Work

I am trained in an integrative therapeutic approach, drawing on Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT), Emotion-Focused Therapy, and Bowen's Family Systems Theory. This means I tailor my approach to your individual needs rather than following a single fixed method.

Sessions are held online via Google Meet, by appointment only, and are 50 minutes in duration. Sessions are typically offered on a weekly basis, though we can discuss the frequency that best suits your needs.

Duration and Ending Therapy

I offer both short and long-term therapy. We will discuss the planned duration of our work together during our initial consultation. If you wish to end therapy, I ask that you let me know as early as possible. I recommend at least one or two closing sessions to bring our work to a considered end.

Cancellation Policy

I require a minimum of 48 hours' notice to cancel or reschedule a session. Cancellations made with less than 48 hours' notice are non-refundable. Late arrivals cannot be accommodated beyond the scheduled end time of the session.

Holidays and Availability

My normal working hours are Monday to Friday, 9am to 7pm UK time. If you need to take a break from sessions, please let me know as early as possible. I will give you a minimum of one session's notice of my own holidays.

Contact Between Sessions

I work with clients only within scheduled sessions. I am not available for therapeutic contact between appointments. For administrative matters (bookings, cancellations), you may contact me at roy@therapywithroy.co.uk or 07824 426000 during normal working hours.

If you are in crisis outside of sessions, please contact:

  • NHS 111 (call or visit 111.nhs.uk)
  • Samaritans: 116 123 (free, 24/7)
  • Emergency services: 999

Supervision

As a BACP Registered Member, I attend regular clinical supervision to ensure my practice is safe, ethical, and effective. Aspects of our work may be discussed in supervision in anonymised form. Your full name and any identifying details will never be disclosed. My supervisor is bound by the same professional standards of confidentiality as I am.

Confidentiality

Everything we discuss in sessions is confidential, with the following exceptions:

  • Harm to yourself or others: If I have serious concerns that you or someone else is at risk of significant harm, I may need to break confidentiality. Where possible, I will discuss this with you before taking action.
  • Legal and ethical obligations: I am not bound by confidentiality where disclosure may assist in the prevention or detection of serious crime, including safeguarding concerns relating to children or vulnerable adults, terrorism, or substantial financial crime.

Record Keeping and Data Protection

I keep brief, accurate session notes stored on an encrypted, password-protected laptop. Paper notes are destroyed by cross-cut shredding once transferred to secure digital storage. Client records are retained for 7 years from the date of our last session, in line with BACP guidance.

I am registered with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO Registration No. ZC100652). All personal data is processed in accordance with UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018. See my full Privacy Policy for more detail.

Complaints

If you have any concerns about our work together, I hope you will feel able to raise them with me directly. If you feel unable to do so, or in the event of a serious complaint, you may contact BACP's Get Help with Counselling Concerns service:

  • Phone: 01455 883300
  • Email: gethelp@bacp.co.uk
  • Post: BACP, 15 St John's Business Park, Lutterworth, LE17 4HB
